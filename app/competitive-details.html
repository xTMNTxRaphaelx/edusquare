Competitive Exams

JEE ( Joint Entrance Examination)

Joint Entrance Examination (JEE) is an all India common engineering entrance examination conducted for admission to various engineering colleges and courses all over the country. It was introduced in 2013. The test comprises two stages - JEE Main and the JEE Advanced. The exams are of the objective pattern. JEE Advanced is regarded internationally as one of the most challenging engineering admission tests.

The ministry of Human Resource Development, Goverment of India has notified following two changes in the JEE patterns for 2017.

  1. There shall be no weightage for the 12th class marks in calcolating the ranks in the JEE mains Examination,
  2. For the candidates to qualify for the admission in the IITs/ NIITs/IIITs and such other CFTIs whose admissions are based on the JEE(advanced/ JEE(Mains) ranks, should have secured at least 75% marks in the 12th class examination, or be in the top 20 percentile in the 12th class examination conducted by the respective boards, for SC/ST students, the qualifying marks would be 65% in the 12th class examination.

JEE (mains) is conducted by CBSE/ MHRD and JEE (advance ) is conducted by joint admission board of the IITs. JEE (mains) is conducted in the month of April in both offline and online mmode, whereas the JEE(advance) is conducted afterwards.

JEE Mains

The online application process generally starts from the december first week and goes on for aroudn a month.As per standard practice, the exam is conducted in offline mode on first sunday of April. It is conducted in other Online Mode on 2 or 3 dates till Mid- April. The result is generlally declared by April- End or May - Beginning.

JEE Advanced

The online application process starts immediately after JEE (Mains) reuslts declaration and goes on for 4-5 days. The Paper is conducted in the third or fourth week of May in offline Mode only, The final result is generally declared in the second week of june.

NEET(National Eligibility and Entrance Test- NEET)

As per the directives of hon'ble Supreme Court of India and the ordinances promulgated by the central goverment, NATIONAL ELIGIBILTY CUM ENTRANCE TEST(UG), 2017 will be conducted by the CBSE, Delhi for the admission to MBBS/ BDS courses in Medical and Dental colleges run with the approval of the Medical Council of India / Dental Council of India under the Union Ministry of Health and Family Welfare , Goverment of India.

The responsibilty of the CBSE is limited to the conduct of the entrance examination, Declaration of the result and providing All India Rank to the counselling Authorities and Admitting institutions.

For more information on eligibility criterion, exam structure, no. of seats and other details, please visit the official site: http://aipmt.nic.in

Neet UG

The National Eligibility-cum-Entrance Test (NEET) would be conducted as a single entrance exam for the admissions undergraduate medical and dental courses. As per the standard Practice, it is safe to say that NEET woulde be conducted on the first Sunday of May.

The application process for NEET starts from the second week of December. Over 50 thousand MBBS and BDS seats would be filled through NEET 2017 as this year all the private and goverment-run institutions would come under the ambit of NEET . Only AIIMs and JIPMER will continue to hold their separate entrance tests as these are the 'Institutes of National importance.' Once started , the application process would continue for at least a month.

BITSAT

The online application process generally starts towards the end of december month and goes ontill end of February month. The Exam is conducting in online mode only, generally in the second and third week of May

AIIMS

The application process for AIIMS undergraduate courses is expected to start in mid-February.

The tentative date for the MBBS entrance test is last Sunday of May& the tentative date of result declaration is mid- June.

JIPMER

Online Application Registration for JIPMER MBBS courses starts from last week of March and closes around April- end or first week of May. The exam is generally conducted on the first sunday of June.
